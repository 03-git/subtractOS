RECOGNIZE TECH SUPPORT SCAMS

Steps:
1. KNOW THE PATTERNS:
   - A popup or full-screen browser alert says your computer is infected and gives a phone number to call. This is always a scam.
   - Someone calls claiming to be from Microsoft, Apple, or your ISP saying your computer has a virus. These companies do not make unsolicited calls.
   - Someone asks you to install remote access software (AnyDesk, TeamViewer) so they can "fix" your computer. Legitimate support does not cold-call and ask for remote access.
   - They ask for payment in gift cards, cryptocurrency, or wire transfer. Legitimate companies never do this.

2. IF YOU SEE A SCARY POPUP:
   - Do not call the number.
   - Close the browser. If you can't close it: force-quit (Cmd+Q on Mac, Ctrl+Alt+Del > End Task on Windows).
   - Clear your browser cache. The popup came from a website, not a virus.

3. IF YOU ALREADY GAVE ACCESS:
   - Disconnect from the internet immediately (unplug ethernet or turn off wifi).
   - Uninstall any software they told you to install.
   - Change passwords for banking and email from a different device.
   - Run a legitimate malware scan (Malwarebytes free version).
   - Contact your bank if you gave payment information.

Notes:
- There is no scenario where Microsoft or Apple will call you about your computer.
- If you're unsure about a real tech issue, contact the company through their official website — never through a number from a popup or unsolicited call/email.
