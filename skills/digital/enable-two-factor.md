ENABLE TWO-FACTOR AUTHENTICATION

Steps:
1. Install an authenticator app: Authy, Google Authenticator, or Microsoft Authenticator. These generate time-based codes on your device.
2. Start with your most critical accounts: email first, then banking, then social media.
3. For each account:
   - Go to security/account settings.
   - Find "Two-factor authentication" or "Two-step verification."
   - Choose "Authenticator app" (preferred over SMS when available).
   - Scan the QR code shown with your authenticator app.
   - Enter the 6-digit code from the app to confirm.
4. SAVE THE BACKUP/RECOVERY CODES. Every service gives you one-time recovery codes. Store these somewhere safe and offline (printed, in a safe, or in your password manager). Without them, you can be permanently locked out.
5. Avoid SMS-based 2FA if an authenticator app option exists. SMS can be intercepted via SIM-swapping attacks.

Notes:
- If you lose your phone without backup codes, account recovery is difficult and sometimes impossible. Save those codes.
- Hardware security keys (YubiKey) are the strongest 2FA option for high-value accounts.
- Do NOT disable 2FA to "make logging in easier." The minor inconvenience prevents the major inconvenience of being hacked.
