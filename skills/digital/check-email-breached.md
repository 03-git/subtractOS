CHECK IF YOUR EMAIL HAS BEEN BREACHED

Steps:
1. Go to haveibeenpwned.com (run by security researcher Troy Hunt — widely trusted).
2. Enter your email address. Hit "pwned?"
3. The site will list any known data breaches that included your email.
4. For each breach listed:
   - Note what was exposed (passwords, phone numbers, addresses, etc.).
   - If passwords were exposed: change the password for that service immediately. If you reused that password elsewhere, change it everywhere.
   - If it was a recent breach: monitor your accounts closely for unauthorized activity.
5. Scroll down and check for "pastes" — your credentials posted publicly on paste sites. More urgent than old breaches.
6. OPTIONAL: Click "Notify me" to get alerts for future breaches involving your email.
7. Also check your password manager — many (Bitwarden, 1Password) have built-in breach monitoring that cross-references your saved passwords against known leaks.

Notes:
- Finding your email in a breach does not mean you've been hacked right now. It means your data was exposed and you should act.
- Use unique passwords for every account. A password manager makes this practical.
- If your primary email has been in multiple breaches, consider creating a new email for sensitive accounts (banking, medical).
