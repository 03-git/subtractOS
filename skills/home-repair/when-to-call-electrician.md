WHEN TO CALL AN ELECTRICIAN (DECISION TREE)

Call immediately — do not attempt:
- Burning smell from an outlet, switch, or panel with no visible source.
- Sparks or arcing from an outlet or panel.
- A breaker trips instantly with nothing plugged in (short in wiring).
- Any part of the electrical panel is warm, discolored, or makes buzzing/crackling sounds.
- Water has entered the electrical panel or any outlet box.
- Lights flicker throughout the house (not just one circuit).
- You received a shock from a switch or outlet.

Call soon — not a DIY job:
- Aluminum wiring (pre-1976 homes, silver-colored wire). Requires special connectors.
- Adding a new circuit or subpanel.
- Any work requiring a permit: new outlets, panel upgrades, EV charger install, hot tub wiring.
- Outlets that are warm to the touch.
- Two-prong outlets you want to upgrade to three-prong (requires grounding evaluation).
- Knob-and-tube wiring (very old homes). Requires professional evaluation before any work.
- Frequent AFCI breaker trips after checking for loose connections.
- Federal Pacific or Zinsco panel (known defective brands). Replace the panel.

Fix it yourself:
- Tripped breaker (one-time) → see: reset-circuit-breaker
- Replacing a switch (standard, with confirmed power off) → see: replace-light-switch
- Circuit overload diagnosis → see: diagnose-circuit-tripping

Notes:
- Electrical work kills. If you are uncertain at any point, stop and call a licensed electrician.
- Unlicensed electrical work may void your homeowner's insurance and violate code.
- A permit is cheap insurance: the inspector catches mistakes before they cause a fire.
