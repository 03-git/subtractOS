UNSTICK A JAMMED GARBAGE DISPOSAL

Steps:
1. Turn the disposal switch OFF. Never put your hand in a running or potentially running disposal.
2. Check the bottom of the unit for a red RESET button. If it's popped out, press it in. Try the switch. If it works, you're done.
3. If the motor hums but doesn't spin: it's jammed. Turn the switch OFF.
4. Find the hex wrench that came with the disposal (1/4 inch Allen key). Insert it into the hex socket on the bottom center of the unit.
5. Turn the wrench back and forth to manually rotate the grinding plate and free the jam.
6. If no hex socket or no wrench: insert a wooden broom handle into the disposal from the top. Push against the impeller blades to rotate them. Never use metal tools.
7. Use tongs or pliers to remove the object causing the jam (bones, utensils, glass).
8. Press the reset button. Run cold water. Turn on the disposal.

Notes:
- Never put your hand inside the disposal, even when it's off and unplugged.
- If the reset button pops out repeatedly, the motor is overheating. Wait 15 minutes between attempts.
- If the motor makes no sound at all (no hum): check the breaker, then check if the unit is plugged in (usually under the sink).
- Call a plumber if: the disposal leaks from the bottom (seal failure) or the motor is completely dead after reset and breaker check.
