REPLACE A LIGHT SWITCH

Steps:
1. Turn off the breaker controlling the switch. Verify power is off by flipping the switch — light should not come on.
2. Remove the cover plate (1-2 screws). Use a non-contact voltage tester on the wires inside the box. Confirm no voltage.
3. Unscrew the switch from the box (2 screws, top and bottom).
4. Pull the switch out gently. Note which wires connect where. Take a photo.
5. Standard switch has 2 brass screws (hot wires) and 1 green screw (ground). A 3-way switch has 3 brass/black screws plus ground — label the wire on the "common" terminal (different colored screw) before disconnecting.
6. Loosen screws. Remove wires. If wires are backstabbed (pushed into holes), insert a small flathead into the release slot.
7. Connect wires to the new switch in the same configuration. Hook wire clockwise around screws. Tighten firmly.
8. Push switch back into box. Screw in. Attach cover plate.
9. Turn breaker on. Test.

Notes:
- If you see aluminum wiring (silver colored), stop. Aluminum requires special switches rated AL/CU. Call an electrician if unsure.
- Always use a voltage tester, never trust the breaker label alone.
- If the box has more than 4 wires or multiple switches, photograph everything before touching anything.
- Call an electrician if: you find aluminum wiring, the box is overcrowded, or you need to run new wire.
