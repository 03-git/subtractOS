FIX A LEAKY FAUCET

Steps:
1. Turn off the water supply valves under the sink (clockwise).
2. Open the faucet to release remaining pressure and water.
3. Determine faucet type: compression (two handles, twist), ball (single handle, swivels), cartridge (single handle, tilts), or ceramic disc (single handle, very smooth).

Steps — Compression Faucet:
4. Remove handle (pry off cap, remove screw). Unscrew the packing nut with a wrench.
5. Pull out the stem. Replace the rubber seat washer at the bottom (held by a brass screw).
6. Reassemble. If still leaking, the valve seat is damaged — resurface it with a seat grinding tool or call a plumber.

Steps — Cartridge/Ball/Disc Faucet:
4. Remove handle. Pull out the cartridge, ball assembly, or disc cylinder.
5. Take the removed part to a hardware store. Buy the exact replacement kit for your faucet brand/model (usually printed under the handle cap or on the faucet body).
6. Install new cartridge/ball/disc per the kit instructions. Replace all O-rings and seals included in the kit.
7. Reassemble. Turn water on slowly. Check for leaks.

Notes:
- Always replace O-rings while you have the faucet apart.
- If the faucet model is unknown, photos and measurements help the hardware store identify it.
- Call a plumber if: the leak is under the sink (supply line), the valve seat is corroded, or the faucet body itself is cracked.
