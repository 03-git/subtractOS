UNCLOG A DRAIN (SINK AND TOILET)

Steps — Sink:
1. Remove the stopper or drain cover. Pull out any visible hair/debris.
2. Boil a kettle of water. Pour it slowly down the drain. Wait 5 minutes.
3. If still clogged: use a cup plunger. Fill sink with 2 inches of water. Plunge vertically 15-20 times with sharp strokes.
4. If still clogged: use a drain snake. Feed it in while turning clockwise. Pull debris out, don't push it deeper.
5. Reassemble stopper. Run water 2 minutes to confirm flow.

Steps — Toilet:
1. Do not flush again if water is high. Wait 10 minutes for water level to drop.
2. Use a flange plunger (has an extended rubber lip). Submerge it fully in water.
3. Push down slowly to seat the plunger, then pull up sharply. Repeat 15-20 times.
4. If plunger fails: use a toilet auger. Feed cable in while cranking handle clockwise. Retract slowly.
5. Flush to confirm. If water drains but slowly, repeat auger.

Notes:
- Never use chemical drain cleaner on a toilet. It damages wax seals and porcelain.
- Chemical drain cleaners corrode old pipes. Mechanical clearing is always safer.
- If a snake hits something solid and immovable, stop. That's likely the pipe itself.
- Call a plumber if: drain backs up into other fixtures, multiple drains clog simultaneously, or sewage smell accompanies the clog.
