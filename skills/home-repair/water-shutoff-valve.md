FIND AND FIX A WATER SHUTOFF VALVE

Steps — Find It:
1. Fixture shutoffs: look directly below sinks (wall or floor), behind toilets (wall), or behind washing machines. These are small oval or round handles.
2. Main shutoff: usually where the water line enters the house. Check the basement/crawlspace wall facing the street, near the water heater, or in a utility closet.
3. If no main shutoff inside: check the meter box at the curb. You need a meter key (available at hardware stores) to open the cover and turn the valve.

Steps — Operate It:
1. Gate valve (round wheel handle): turn clockwise to close. May require several full turns.
2. Ball valve (lever handle): turn 90 degrees so the handle is perpendicular to the pipe.
3. If stuck: apply penetrating oil (PB Blaster or WD-40). Wait 15 minutes. Use an adjustable wrench on the handle for leverage. Turn slowly — do not force.
4. After closing, open the lowest faucet in the house to confirm water stops.

Steps — Replace a Bad Valve:
1. Shut off water upstream of the bad valve (main shutoff or curb).
2. This requires cutting pipe and soldering or using push-fit (SharkBite) fittings.
3. Unless you have soldering or push-fit experience, call a plumber.

Notes:
- Know your main shutoff location BEFORE an emergency. Walk it now.
- Gate valves fail frequently. If yours is original to the house, plan to replace it with a ball valve.
- Call a plumber if: the valve breaks off, won't stop the water, or you can't locate it.
