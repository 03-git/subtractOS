TEST AND REPLACE SMOKE DETECTOR BATTERY

Steps — Test:
1. Press and hold the test button on the detector for 3 seconds. It should sound a loud alarm.
2. If no sound or weak sound: replace the battery.
3. Test every detector in the house. Do this monthly.

Steps — Replace Battery:
1. Twist the detector counterclockwise to remove it from the mounting plate. Some models pull straight down or have a latch.
2. Open the battery compartment. Remove the old battery.
3. Insert a fresh 9V battery (most common) or AA/lithium (check the compartment). Match polarity markings.
4. Close compartment. Press test button to confirm loud alarm.
5. Remount the detector on the ceiling plate.

Steps — Detector Still Chirps After New Battery:
1. Remove the battery. Hold the test button to drain residual charge.
2. Reinsert the battery. Test again.
3. If chirping persists: the detector is expired. Check the manufacture date on the back. Smoke detectors expire after 10 years. Replace the entire unit.

Notes:
- Replace all batteries once per year (pick a fixed date: daylight saving change or a birthday).
- Hardwired detectors also have backup batteries. Replace them the same way.
- If a detector is older than 10 years, replace the whole unit regardless of function.
- Install detectors in every bedroom, outside each sleeping area, and on every level.
