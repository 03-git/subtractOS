CLEAN DRYER LINT TRAP AND VENT

Steps — Lint Trap (every load):
1. Pull the lint screen out. Peel lint off by hand.
2. Monthly: wash the screen with soap and water using a brush. Dryer sheet residue builds an invisible film that blocks airflow. Let it dry fully before reinserting.

Steps — Vent Duct (every 6-12 months):
1. Pull the dryer away from the wall. Unplug it. If gas dryer, do not disconnect the gas line — just pull out enough to access the vent.
2. Disconnect the flexible duct from the back of the dryer (loosen the clamp or squeeze the squeeze-ring).
3. Use a dryer vent brush kit (long flexible rod with a brush head). Feed it into the duct from the dryer end. Spin and push through to the exterior vent.
4. Go outside. Remove the vent cover. Brush from the outside in if accessible.
5. Vacuum all lint from both ends and the area behind the dryer.
6. Reconnect the duct. Push the dryer back. Plug in.
7. Run the dryer on air-only for 5 minutes. Go outside and confirm strong airflow at the exterior vent.

Notes:
- Clogged dryer vents are a leading cause of house fires.
- Replace crushed or kinked flexible foil ducts with rigid or semi-rigid metal duct.
- If the exterior vent flap doesn't open during operation, the duct is still blocked.
- Long vent runs or runs with multiple elbows: hire a vent cleaning service.
