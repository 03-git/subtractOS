PATCH A SMALL DRYWALL HOLE

Steps — Nail/Screw Holes (under 1/4 inch):
1. Press lightweight spackle into the hole with a putty knife.
2. Scrape flush. Let dry 30 minutes.
3. Sand lightly with 120-grit sandpaper. Prime and paint.

Steps — Small Holes (1/4 inch to 4 inches):
1. Clean ragged edges with a utility knife.
2. Apply a self-adhesive mesh patch over the hole.
3. Spread joint compound over the patch with a 6-inch putty knife. Extend 2 inches past the patch edges. Let dry per label (usually 24 hours).
4. Apply a second thin coat. Feather the edges out further. Let dry.
5. Sand with 120-grit until smooth and flush with the wall.
6. Prime with PVA primer. Paint.

Steps — Medium Holes (4 to 6 inches):
1. Cut a clean square around the hole with a drywall saw.
2. Cut a drywall piece slightly larger than the square. Score and snap to exact size.
3. Screw a thin wood backer strip behind the opening. Screw the patch to the backer.
4. Tape seams with mesh tape. Apply joint compound. Dry, second coat, dry, sand, prime, paint.

Notes:
- Match paint: bring a chip to the store or use the code on the original can.
- For holes larger than 6 inches or holes near electrical/plumbing, call a handyman or check for wires before cutting.
